Captain Randy "Carp" Carpenter


Coos Bay Fire Department

A career firefighter, Captain Randy E. Carpenter, 46, died November 25, 2002, when the burning roof of a business in downtown Coos Bay collapsed. Randy had been a firefighter for a total of 23 years. He began his career as a volunteer, and later became a full-time firefighter with Baker City Fire Department. In February 1989, he accepted a position with Coos Bay Fire Department and his dedication there truly established his legacy. He was Baker City's 1983 'Firefighter of the Year', and Coos Bay's 1991 'Firefighter of the Year'. He was also Coos Bay"s 1999 'EMT of the Year'.

Randy's interest in firefighting began early in his life when he would accompany his father, a volunteer firefighter for Baker City for 46 years, to the firestation. During the time he was a volunteer, Randy was very active in the Oregon Volunteer Firefighter Association and served in all State Association offices, including president. Randy was a consummate professional and was highly qualified in all operational and procedural aspects of his job. He was part of the original efforts to bring a hazardous materials unit to Coos Bay and was a specialist in HAZMAT. He was qualified and certified to teach all subjects of firefighting and regularly taught courses in CPR and EMT Basic at the local college in Coos Bay. He took great pride in his job and treasured the trust and respect of all his firefighter brethren. He was an active member of the International Association of Firefighters, IAFF, Local 2935 (serving as President in 1991), and the Regional Hazardous Materials Team. He was also skilled in construction and built a successful contracting business with his good friend Jim Bush.

The love of family was very foremost in Randy's life. He was so proud of and totally dedicated to his two girls, Sarah and Stephanie, with priority in providing for their needs. His parents, Wayne and Dru, three brothers, Kerry, Brad, and Jeff, their spouses and families also survived him.

Randy was usually very successful when he took his boat out "crabbing", and was always happy when he could take time to go water-skiing, at which he was very adept. He especially loved spending time in Eastern Oregon (Dad & Mom referred to him as their "homing pigeon") where he would hunt deer, elk, and antelope, along with working with longtime friends on the Bunch Ranch near Durkee. While Randy had a wonderful sense of humor and enjoyed playing a practical joke, he never wanted the limelight, just a way to be of help. His honesty and integrity were unquestionable. All his family and his countless friends, who were like family to him, will miss Randy beyond description.

The Randy Carpenter Memorial Firefighter Training Fund has been established to provide funds to aid in needed training for Oregon and Southwest Idaho firefighters and emergency personnel.

The Randy Carpenter Memorial Foundation


HISTORY OF THE FOUNDATION

On November 25th of 2002 the community of Coos Bay OR. suffered a tragic loss when three of their local firefighters lost their lives in the line of duty. Randy Carpenter, Jeff Common, and Chuck Hanners all gave their lives as heroes serving their community while fighting a fire in a local auto parts store. After the roof of the store collapsed, the fire was extinguished, and the smoke had cleared, three families and a community mourned their great loss.

Of course, as with any accident, many questions arose, most asked without answer. Was there something that could have been done to prevent this horrible event? In response, the family and friends of Randy Carpenter have established a foundation in memory of their son, brother, father, uncle, and friend. Everything we do is done with the hopes of helping to prevent future tragic loss of firefighter's lives.

Randy believed strongly in training and would often state, "Preparation is the separation between a good firefighter and the best!" Continuing in this belief the Randy Carpenter Memorial Foundation is dedicated to the education and preparation of firefighters everywhere.

PURPOSE OF THE FOUNDATION

This site, and the Foundation named in his honor, are dedicated to the memory and vision of our Fallen Brother, Capt. Randy Carpenter, Coos Bay Fire and Rescue. Randy’s dedication and selfless commitment to helping train his fire brethren will forever serve as our call to action.

As training budgets get slashed or become non-existent, our Mission becomes even more critical! Every penny raised by the RCMF is granted back to Firefighters for critical hands on training, training assessment, or specialized education. The RCMF provides information and training that otherwise might not be available or utilized. To date (2014), thanks to the selfless efforts of our Members and the incredible support shown by our Participants, the RCMF has provided over $200,000 in training and education to hundreds of Firefighters in Oregon and Western Idaho.

Randy worked tirelessly so that after the rescue was made…the fire out…the job done or the shift over; every Firefighter went home safely. We of the Randy Carpenter Memorial Foundation aspire to continue Randy’s commitment. Thank you for assisting us in carrying on Randy’s legacy.

Learn more, practice often, train always and BE SAFE! Firefighters will ALWAYS be there for us! Let’s be there for THEM!

Request for Training Grant

The Randy Carpenter Memorial Foundation will review all requests for training grants that are submitted to the foundation. Trainings that meet the initial critera for funding by the foundation will be put forward for a vote of the Board of Directors of the RCMF.

To apply for a grant you should send via email an attached request for funds on the letterhead of the department or organization that will be providing the training. Please include a discription of the training and how it will provide training or improved skills directly to firefighters. Please include the amount that you are requesting from the foundation as well as a budget for your training event for review by the Board of Directors.
